London dry gin · honey syrup · lemon juice

Ingredients

  • 2 oz London dry gin
  • 1 oz honey syrup
  • 1 oz lemon juice
  • Lemon wedge (garnish)

Method

Pour lemon juice, honey syrup, and gin into a shaker. Crack ice and shake until well chilled.

Corpse Reviver #2
Gin
RefreshingHerbalClassic
Gin · dry curaçao · Cocchi Americano · lemon · absinthe rinse

Ingredients

  • ¾ oz gin
  • ¾ oz dry curaçao
  • ¾ oz Cocchi Americano
  • ¾ oz lemon juice
  • Absinthe (for rinse)
  • Lemon peel (garnish)

Method

Spritz the inside of a chilled Nick & Nora glass with absinthe and set aside.

Combine remaining ingredients in a shaker. Add ice and shake until well chilled. Strain into the prepared glass and garnish with a lemon peel.

A pre-Prohibition classic, originally prescribed as a hangover cure. Cocchi Americano is essential here — it's what gives the drink its distinctive floral bitterness. Don't overdo the absinthe rinse.

Dark rum · ginger beer · lime

Ingredients

  • 2 oz dark rum
  • 4 oz ginger beer
  • ½ oz lime juice
  • Lime wedge (garnish)

Method

Fill a highball glass with ice. Pour in lime juice and ginger beer, then float the dark rum on top by pouring it slowly over the back of a bar spoon.

Espresso Martini
Vodka
SweetBoozyModern
Vodka · coffee liqueur · espresso · simple syrup

Ingredients

  • 2 oz vodka
  • ½ oz coffee liqueur
  • ½ oz simple syrup
  • 1 double shot of espresso
  • 3 whole coffee beans (garnish)

Method

Pour espresso, simple syrup, coffee liqueur, and vodka into a shaker. Add cracked ice and shake until well chilled.

Strain out the ice, then dry shake again vigorously to build the foam.

Pour into a chilled coupe and garnish with coffee beans.

Pull the espresso fresh and let it cool slightly before shaking — hot liquid and ice don't mix well. The dry shake after straining is what gives you that signature creamy top.

Piña Colada
Rum
SweetRefreshingClassic
White rum · Jamaican rum · frozen pineapple · crème of coconut

Ingredients

  • 450g frozen pineapple chunks
  • 250g ice
  • 3 oz crème of coconut
  • 3 oz pineapple juice
  • 2 oz white rum
  • 2 oz Jamaican rum
  • 1 oz fresh lime juice
  • 4 dashes Angostura bitters
  • Maraschino cherries (garnish)

Method

Combine all ingredients in a blender and blend until smooth and frappéd.

Let the mixture rest for 5 minutes — this allows it to thicken slightly. Pour into chilled mugs or glasses and garnish with cherries.

Makes 2 servings. Crème of coconut (such as Coco López) is sweetened — don't substitute coconut cream or coconut milk.

Blended scotch · lemon · honey syrup · ginger · Islay float

Ingredients

  • 2 oz blended scotch
  • ¾ oz lemon juice
  • ½ oz honey syrup
  • ½ oz ginger syrup
  • ½ oz Islay single malt (float)
  • Candied or sliced ginger (garnish)

Method

Pour blended scotch, lemon juice, honey syrup, and ginger syrup into a shaker. Add cracked ice and shake well.

Add a large ice cube to a whiskey glass. Strain in the cocktail. Float the Islay scotch on top. Garnish with ginger.

Best with Monkey Shoulder (blended) and Laphroaig (Islay float). Ginger syrup: blend equal parts by volume rough-chopped ginger (no need to peel), boiling water, and sugar until smooth. Strain through a cheesecloth and refrigerate.

Whiskey Sour
Bourbon / Rye
BalancedClassic
Bourbon · lemon juice · simple syrup · egg white foam

Ingredients

  • 2 oz bourbon
  • 1 oz lemon juice
  • 1 oz simple syrup
  • White of 1 egg
  • 3 dashes Angostura bitters (garnish)

Method

Pour lemon juice, simple syrup, bourbon, and egg white into a shaker. Dry shake until frothy.

Add cracked ice and shake again until well chilled and frothy. Double strain into a chilled coupe.

Add three dashes of Angostura bitters on the foam and use a toothpick to create a design.

Shake very well — the foam is the signature of this drink.
